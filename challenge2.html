--- June 18th: Rosetta Kibble ---

It's Father's Day, and you're a father.

As you make your way through your stack of cards, you come across a Father's Day card from your children. The handwriting is so messy that all you can make out are the first few words, "Your gift awaits you by the *#%0aiohr*#$(*#@)..."

Pepper comes up to you and puts her paws on your legs. It seems like she is trying to tell you something. Perhaps she knows something about where your gift is located! You follow her, and she takes you to her food bowl, where kibble is scattered all over the floor.

Although Pepper is unable to talk, maybe she could tell you where your gift is hidden by rearranging the kibble. Unfortunately, Pepper does not have opposable thumbs, so she does not have the precision to move the kibble herself. After a few minutes, you notice that Pepper is using her nose to tell you how to move the kibble.

Consider the following arrangement of kibble:

H000000
0123450
0000000

A 0 represents an empty tile on the floor, that is, a floor tile with no kibble on it. A 1 represents a tile on the floor with one piece of kibble, and so on. No floor tile has more than nine pieces of kibble on it.

You decide, arbitratily, that you will start by holding your hand above the top left tile on the floor, indicated above by the letter H. Using her nose, Pepper is able to issue you five different commands:

  • up: Pepper tilts her head upward to signify that you should move your hand one tile upwards (from a bird's eye perspective)
  • down: Pepper tilts her head downward to signify that you should move your hand one tile downwards
  • left: Pepper tilts her head to the left to signify that you should move your hand one tile to the left
  • right: Pepper tilts her head to the right to signify that you should move your hand one tile to the right
  • bark: Pepper wags her tail and barks with excitement, signaling that you should drop a piece of kibble if you are holding one, or pick up one piece of kibble if you aren't.

Pepper is highly intelligent, so she will not try to move your hand outside of the grid of tiles nor will she bark when there is no kibble in your hand and no kibble in the current tile.
